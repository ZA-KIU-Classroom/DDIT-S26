# Weekend 2 Module

## Dates
Saturday April 4, 2026

10:00 to 18:00 both days

## Theme
Generative AI strategy, platform competition, and strategic options for the midterm

## Syllabus alignment
Weekend 2 covers Weeks 5 through 8: Generative AI foundations, autonomous agents and AI platforms, Three Horizons innovation portfolio, and strategic recommendations with ROI modelling.

## What changed since Weekend 1

Teams have completed a three-week sprint. They arrive with:
- hypothesis research summaries with at least four of five hypotheses tested,
- updated competitive landscape (v2),
- three preliminary strategic options identified but not yet evaluated,
- completed pre-reading from Power and Prediction, Platform Revolution, and Competing in the Age of AI.

Teams that cannot present these items in the Saturday morning sprint review have not done the sprint to standard.

## Learning objectives

By the end of Weekend 2, students should be able to:
- explain what large language models can and cannot do at a business level,
- apply the prediction machine framework to identify AI-exposed decisions,
- distinguish between tool-augmented AI, AI-augmented workflows, agentic AI, and autonomous systems,
- apply platform strategy concepts to assess build, join, or defend options,
- map AI use cases to a value chain with business value and readiness scoring,
- evaluate three structurally distinct strategic options on four criteria,
- build an ROI model with three scenarios, stated assumptions, and payback estimates,
- structure a midterm business case outline using the Pyramid Principle.

## How this weekend connects to the midterm

Everything produced this weekend is a direct input to the midterm presentation (25 points, Weekend 3). The midterm is a 25 to 30 slide executive presentation plus a supporting business case document. Teams that leave Weekend 2 with strong deliverables and a credible sprint plan are in a fundamentally better position for the midterm.
