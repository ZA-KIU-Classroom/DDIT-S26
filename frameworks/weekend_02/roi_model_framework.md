# ROI Model Framework for Digital Transformation

## Purpose

This framework structures the financial case for a transformation recommendation. It is designed for the midterm business case.

## Five layers of the model

| Layer | Content | Data sources |
|---|---|---|
| Revenue impact | New revenue from new customers, products, increased ARPU, reduced churn | Industry benchmarks, peer disclosures, competitive landscape research |
| Cost impact | Operating savings from automation, process improvement, headcount reduction | AI Opportunity Map estimates, industry productivity benchmarks |
| Investment required | Total cost: technology, implementation, people, change management, governance, contingency | Vendor pricing, implementation cost ratios |
| Assumptions | Every non-sourced number stated with basis and sensitivity | Label as [A1], [A2] etc. |
| Scenario structure | Base case, upside, downside differentiated on one key assumption | Benchmark range calibration |

## Investment cost proportions

| Category | Typical proportion |
|---|---|
| Technology platform and licensing | 15 to 25% |
| Implementation and integration | 25 to 35% |
| People and change management | 20 to 30% |
| Programme management and governance | 10 to 15% |
| Data infrastructure | 10 to 20% |
| Contingency | 15 to 25% |

## Key financial metrics

| Metric | When it matters most |
|---|---|
| Payback period | Board has capital constraints or short planning horizons |
| NPV | Comparing options with different timing and scale of cash flows |
| IRR | Comparing the return to cost of capital |
| Year 1 cash requirement | Board evaluating the real commitment in the current budget cycle |

## Most common modelling error

Estimating technology cost only and ignoring implementation, change management, and contingency. A transformation model without a change management budget line will be challenged in Q&A.
