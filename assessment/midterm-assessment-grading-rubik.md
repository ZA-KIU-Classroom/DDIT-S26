## Deliverables

The midterm exam consists of two deliverables submitted together.

### Deliverable 1: Executive Presentation (25 to 30 slides)

A boardroom-quality slide deck presenting the team's strategic recommendations and business case. Every slide must have a **headline that states the conclusion of that slide**, not a topic label. The body of the slide provides the evidence supporting that conclusion. This is the Pyramid Principle applied to presentation design.

### Deliverable 2: Supporting Business Case Documentation

A written document that provides the analytical depth behind the presentation. This includes the full ROI model with assumptions, detailed competitive analysis, extended risk assessment, and any supporting data tables or research summaries that do not fit on slides but are essential to defending the recommendation.

## Presentation Structure

The presentation must follow this ten-section structure. Slide counts are guidelines, not hard constraints, but the total must fall between 25 and 30 slides.

| # | Section | Slides | Required Content |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Executive Summary | 1 -- 2 | The three most important conclusions from the entire presentation. The recommendation in one sentence. The ROI headline number. |
| 2 | Industry and Competitive Context | 4 -- 5 | Rogers five domains applied to the industry. Christensen classification of the primary disruption force. Porter Five Forces summary. Key competitive dynamics. |
| 3 | AI Landscape and Opportunity | 3 -- 4 | AI adoption state in the industry. Top three AI use cases from the Opportunity Map. Strategic implications of AI capability trajectory. |
| 4 | Platform Dynamics | 2 -- 3 | Platform competitive structure. Client's current position. Threat assessment from platform entrants. |
| 5 | Strategic Options Evaluation | 4 -- 5 | Three structurally distinct options described clearly. Four-criterion evaluation matrix scored for all options. Recommendation with three supporting reasons. |
| 6 | Business Model Detail | 3 -- 4 | The recommended business model: value proposition, operating model changes, revenue model, data and AI infrastructure requirements. |
| 7 | Transformation Roadmap Overview | 2 -- 3 | Three Horizons portfolio. First 12-month milestones. Key dependencies and sequencing logic. |
| 8 | ROI and Financial Case | 3 -- 4 | Base case financials. Three-scenario payback analysis. Key assumptions listed. Year 1 capital requirement. |
| 9 | Risk Assessment | 2 | Top three risks. One mitigation strategy per risk. Residual risk level after mitigation. |
| 10 | Implementation Logic | 1 -- 2 | Governance structure. Ownership assignments. Key decisions required by the board now. |

## Assessment Criteria

The midterm is evaluated on five criteria. Each criterion is weighted equally within the 25-point allocation.

| Criterion | What the Marker Looks For |
|---|---|
| **Depth of industry and competitive analysis** | Evidence that the team has gone beyond surface-level description. Every technology finding must be followed by a statement beginning "This means that..." Analysis must connect to strategic consequences, not end at description. Frameworks (Rogers, Christensen, Porter) must be applied correctly and specifically to the client industry. |
| **Strategic insight and business model innovation** | Three structurally distinct strategic options (not three intensities of the same direction). A clear recommendation defended with three evidence-based reasons using the Pyramid Principle. The recommended business model must articulate the mechanism of value creation. |
| **Financial rigor and ROI justification** | A complete ROI model with five layers: revenue impact, cost impact, investment required, assumptions table, and scenario structure. Every non-sourced number labelled as an assumption with a stated basis. Three scenarios differentiated on a single named key assumption. Payback period, NPV, and Year 1 capital requirement calculated. |
| **Quality of recommendations and implementation logic** | Recommendations must be specific, defensible, and structured. The transformation roadmap must show dependencies and sequencing logic using the Three Horizons framework. Governance structure and ownership must be defined. |
| **Professional presentation standards** | Conclusion headlines on every slide. Clean visual design. Logical flow from problem to recommendation. Executive summary written first and reflecting the three most important conclusions. No orphan slides, no topic-label-only headlines, no unsupported claims. |

## Strategic Options Framework

Every team must present three structurally distinct strategic options. The three archetypes below are illustrative, not prescriptive. Teams may define their own options as long as they are structurally different from each other.

| Option Type | Definition |
|---|---|
| Defend and Optimise | Invest in AI augmentation of the existing business model without changing the underlying value proposition or revenue model. Protect the current competitive position. |
| Focused Transformation | Selectively restructure one high-value domain of the business model using digital and AI capabilities. Partial platform or data strategy. |
| Full Digital Transformation | Fundamentally redesign the operating model as an AI-first platform business. Highest investment, highest risk, highest long-term return. |

### Evaluation Criteria for Options

Each option must be evaluated on the same four criteria using a consistent matrix. Evaluating Option 1 on financial return but Option 2 on strategic ambition makes the comparison meaningless.

| Criterion | Definition | How to Score |
|---|---|---|
| Strategic fit | How well does this option address the core strategic problem identified in the issue tree? Does it respond to the most important disruption forces? | High: directly addresses the core problem. Medium: addresses secondary dimensions. Low: tangential to the core problem. |
| Financial return | What is the estimated ROI, payback period, and NPV of this option in the base case? | Score relative to the cost of capital and the return available from the status quo. |
| Implementation feasibility | Does the organisation have the capabilities, data assets, change management capacity, and capital required to execute this option? | High: capabilities exist or can be built within the timeline. Medium: significant gaps but addressable. Low: requires capabilities the organisation cannot realistically build. |
| Competitive defensibility | Does this option create a durable competitive advantage that is hard to replicate? | High: structural advantage (network effects, data moats, switching costs). Medium: temporary advantage. Low: easily replicated by competitors. |

## ROI Model Requirements

The ROI model is the financial core of the business case. Every number must have a source or a stated assumption.

### Five Required Layers

| Layer | Content | Data Sources |
|---|---|---|
| Revenue impact | New revenue enabled by the transformation: new customers, new products, increased ARPU, reduced churn | Industry benchmarks; peer company disclosures; competitive landscape research |
| Cost impact | Operating cost savings from automation, process improvement, headcount optimization, infrastructure rationalization | AI Opportunity Map deployment cost estimates; industry productivity benchmarks; value chain analysis |
| Investment required | Total cost of transformation: technology, implementation, people, change management, governance, contingency | Vendor pricing benchmarks; implementation cost ratios from comparable programs |
| Assumptions table | Every non-sourced number labelled [A1], [A2], etc. with its basis and sensitivity | Team research and calibration from benchmark ranges |
| Scenario structure | Base case (most likely), upside (key assumptions at positive end), downside (most important risk materializes) | Differentiated on one key assumption per scenario |

### Required Financial Metrics

| Metric | Purpose |
|---|---|
| Payback period | Primary board screening metric: how long before the investment is recouped from net cash flows |
| Net present value (NPV) | Total value created in today's dollars: captures both the magnitude and timing of cash flows |
| Internal rate of return (IRR) | The return on the transformation investment: must exceed the cost of capital to create value |
| Year 1 cash requirement | The near-term capital commitment: the real commitment in the current budget cycle |
| Steady-state cost structure | The operating cost profile after transformation is complete: demonstrates the structural advantage being created |

### Calibration Benchmarks

Use these benchmarks to calibrate your assumptions, not as your numbers. Apply the range to build downside, base, and upside scenarios.

| Use Case Category | Benchmark Range | Applicability |
|---|---|---|
| AI-powered customer service | 20 to 35% reduction in cost per interaction for routine cases | Banking, healthcare, logistics |
| AI credit scoring and underwriting | 30 to 50% reduction in underwriting cost; 15 to 25% improvement in default prediction accuracy | Banking |
| AI-assisted diagnostics | 10 to 40% reduction in diagnostic time for imaging and pathology | Healthcare |
| Route optimization | 8 to 15% reduction in fuel and vehicle cost; 20 to 30% improvement in on-time delivery | Logistics |
| Document processing automation | 60 to 80% reduction in manual processing time for standardized documents | All industries |
| Customer churn reduction via AI prediction | 10 to 25% reduction in churn rate when combined with proactive retention | Banking, healthcare |

## Five Most Common Midterm Failures

These are not theoretical. They come directly from the assessment criteria and from observation of how teams typically perform.

| Failure | How to Prevent It |
|---|---|
| Industry analysis describes technology without strategic consequences | Every technology finding must be followed by a sentence beginning "This means that..." Do not let analysis end at description. |
| Strategic options are intensities of the same direction | Options must be structurally different: different business model logic, different customer focus, or different operating model architecture. Three versions of "invest more in digital" are not three options. |
| ROI model has no stated assumptions | Every non-sourced number must be labelled as an assumption and listed with a basis. Without this, the model cannot be defended under Q&A. |
| Transformation roadmap is a list of initiatives without sequencing logic | The roadmap must show dependencies: why does Initiative B start after Initiative A and not before? The Three Horizons framework provides this logic. |
| Executive summary is written last and is generic | Write the executive summary first. If you cannot state the three most important conclusions in three sentences, the analysis is not complete. The executive summary is the test of analytical clarity. |

## AI Tool Usage Policy

AI assistants (ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini, Copilot, and similar tools) are permitted as productivity tools throughout this assessment. The following rules apply:

1. **Disclosure is required.** If AI tools were used substantially in analysis, writing, modeling, or slide design, include a brief appendix describing which tools were used and for what purpose.
2. **Understanding is mandatory.** Every team member must be able to explain and defend any content in the presentation, regardless of how it was produced. If a team member cannot explain a slide they are presenting, the examiner will note this.
3. **AI output is not a substitute for judgment.** The strategic recommendation, the evaluation of options, and the financial assumptions must reflect the team's own analytical judgment. AI may assist with research, drafting, and computation, but the strategic choices belong to the team.
